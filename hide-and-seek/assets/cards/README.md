# Card Descriptions — Rural Hide and Seek

Use these descriptions to design and print your own card deck. Cards are the core mechanic of Hide and Seek: Seekers spend **Location Cards** to ask yes/no geographic questions, and Hiders spend **Evasion Cards** to protect their position.

## Location Cards (Seeker Team)

Location Cards allow the Seeker team to ask the Hider team a **yes/no geographic question**. Hiders must answer truthfully. At most one Location Card may be played per 10 minutes.

Questions must be answerable with a clear yes or no based on the current map. Print each card with the question text, or use blank cards and write in the question at game start.

### Suggested Location Card Questions

| # | Card Name | Question |
|---|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **County Check** | Are you currently in [named county]? |
| 2 | **North/South Split** | Are you north of [named highway or river]? |
| 3 | **East/West Split** | Are you east of [named highway or road]? |
| 4 | **Zone 1 Check** | Are you within a 15-minute drive of [named town]? |
| 5 | **Zone 2 Check** | Are you within a 30-minute drive of [named town]? |
| 6 | **Landmark Proximity** | Are you within 10 miles of [named landmark]? |
| 7 | **Small Town** | Are you currently inside an incorporated town or city? |
| 8 | **Tiny Town** | Are you in a town with a population below 500? |
| 9 | **Highway Access** | Are you within 5 miles of a US or state highway? |
| 10 | **Paved Road** | Are you currently on or parked adjacent to a paved road? |
| 11 | **River/Creek** | Are you within 1 mile of a named river or creek? |
| 12 | **Elevation** | Are you at an elevation above [X] feet? *(use topo map or phone altimeter)* |
| 13 | **State Forest / Public Land** | Are you within a state or national forest, wildlife area, or other public land? |
| 14 | **County Seat** | Are you in the county seat of your current county? |
| 15 | **Border Zone** | Are you within 5 miles of a county line? |
| 16 | **Zone Check (Custom)** | Are you within the driving-time zone shown on the game map for [reference point]? |
| 17 | **Cardinal Quadrant** | Are you in the [NE/NW/SE/SW] quadrant of the play area? |
| 18 | **Population Corridor** | Are you within 2 miles of a town with a population above 1,000? |
| 19 | **Gravel Road** | Are you currently parked on or adjacent to an unpaved road? |
| 20 | **Dead Zone** | Are you in an area with no cell service? *(answer by text when service returns)* |

**Customizing Location Cards:** Before the game, fill in specific county names, highway numbers, town names, and landmarks relevant to your actual play area. Generic cards with blanks to write in work well.

## Evasion Cards (Hider Team)

Evasion Cards allow the Hider team to protect their position. Each card is single-use unless noted.

| # | Card Name | Effect |
|---|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **Silence** | Hiders do not have to answer the next Location Card played against them. Seekers do not get a re-draw. |
| 2 | **Misdirect** | Hiders may give a false answer to the next Location Card. *(Single use for the entire game — mark the card when used.)* |
| 3 | **Ghost Mode** | Seekers may not play Location Cards for the next 20 minutes. |
| 4 | **County Lock** | Seekers may not cross into a new county for the next 20 minutes. |
| 5 | **Road Block** | Name any public road. Seekers may not travel on that road for 20 minutes. |
| 6 | **Smallest Town Shield** | While Hiders are inside the smallest incorporated municipality in the play area, Seekers must spend one Range Card before playing a Location Card. *(Lasts until Hiders leave that municipality.)* |
| 7 | **Extra Time** | Add 20 minutes to the game clock in the Hiders' favor. |
| 8 | **Range Drain** | Seekers lose a Quarter Range immediately. |
| 9 | **Decoy** | Hiders may name any town or road as a false current location. Seekers must drive to that location before playing another Location Card. *(Hiders must have been at that location at some point during the game to use this card.)* |
| 10 | **Signal Scramble** | Seekers may not ask locals for information about unfamiliar vehicles for 15 minutes. |

## Range Cards (Both Teams)

Range Cards track each team's driving budget. They are vehicle-agnostic — mileage works whether you drive a gas, diesel, hybrid, or electric vehicle.

Print these at a smaller size (business card or index card) and use as tokens.

| Card | Mileage Value | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Quarter Range | ~100 miles | Most common denomination |
| Half Range | ~200 miles | |
| Full Range | ~400 miles | Used at game start |
| Range Boost | +50 miles | Bonus card |

*Adjust mileage values to reflect your vehicle's actual range and the size of your play area.*

## County-Crossing Cards

Print one card per county boundary in your play area. Customize the name before printing:

> **[COUNTY A] ↔ [COUNTY B] Crossing Card**  
> *Play this card to legally cross the boundary between [County A] and [County B].*  
> *Each card is single-use.*
