# Range Budgeting

> Part of the [Rural Hide and Seek Ruleset](./README.md)

---

## Core Change: Transit Passes → Range Cards

In urban Hide and Seek, players spend a budget on transit tickets and passes. In the rural variant, **driving range is the primary resource**. Players draw and spend **Range Cards** to move, and must manage their vehicle's range carefully. Range Cards are vehicle-agnostic — they represent miles of driving whether your vehicle runs on gasoline, diesel, electricity, or anything else.

---

## Range Card System

### Card Values
Each Range Card represents a mileage allowance. Suggested denominations:

| Card | Mileage Value |
|------|---------------|
| Quarter Range | ~100 miles of driving |
| Half Range | ~200 miles of driving |
| Full Range | ~400 miles of driving |
| Range Boost | +50 miles (bonus card) |

Adjust mileage values to reflect your play area size and expected driving.

### Starting Allocation
Each team begins the game with Range Cards totaling approximately **400 miles** (one Full Range card or equivalent).

- This is enough to explore a 50–100 mile play area for 4–6 hours.
- For longer games or larger areas, increase starting allocation to **600–800 miles**.

### Spending Range Cards
- Before driving any leg, the active team announces the destination.
- Deduct the estimated mileage from their current Range Card balance.
- If a team exhausts their Range Cards mid-game, they must draw a **Breakdown Card** (see Budget Events below) or forfeit that turn.

---

## Cost Considerations

### Why Mileage Instead of Dollars?
Fuel and energy costs vary by vehicle type and fluctuate over time. Using mileage rather than dollar amounts keeps the game fair regardless of what kind of vehicle players are driving or when the game is played.

### Vehicle Wear & Tear (Optional Rule)
For multi-day games, introduce a **Maintenance Token** system:
- Each team starts with 3 Maintenance Tokens.
- Driving on gravel or unpaved roads costs 1 token per 10 miles.
- Running out of tokens means the team must "visit a mechanic" (sit out one turn).

---

## Budget Events

These events can be triggered by Evasion Cards or scenario cards:

| Event | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Range Bonus** | Draw one free Range Card |
| **Detour Penalty** | The affected team loses a Quarter Range immediately |
| **Range Loss** | Lose a Half Range immediately |
| **Free Charge** | Seekers lose a Half Range (Hider found a free fueling or charging station) |
| **Breakdown** | Sit out for 10 game-minutes; draw a Quarter Range to continue |
| **Carpool** | Two teams must travel together for the next 20 game-minutes |

---

## Example Budget Calculation

**Play area:** 3 counties, roughly 60-mile radius  
**Game duration:** 5 hours  
**Estimated driving per team:** 150–250 miles  
**Recommended starting allocation:** Half Range + Quarter Range (300 miles total)
