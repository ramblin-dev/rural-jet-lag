## Overview

Rural Jet Lag: Hide and Seek is a fan-made adaptation of Jet Lag: The Game's Hide and Seek format for rural environments. Players split into **Hiders** and **Seekers**. The Hiders pick a secret location anywhere in the play area and stay there. The Seekers use **Location Cards** to ask yes/no geographic questions that progressively narrow down where the Hiders are. Hiders use **Evasion Cards** to dodge questions and buy time.

**Players:** 4–8 (2+ per team; minimum 2 teams)  
**Duration:** 3–8 hours  
**Play Area:** 25–100 mile radius from a central starting point  

---

## Setup

### 1. Define the Play Area
- Agree on a central starting point (a small town center or well-known landmark).
- Define the outer boundary: county line(s), a circle of agreed radius, or named roads/highways.
- Mark prohibited zones (private property, etc.) on a shared map.
- Share the map with all players. Use Google My Maps, a printed county map, or a GPS app.

### 2. Assign Teams
- **Hider Team:** 1 vehicle, 2+ players. Starts 30 minutes before Seekers.
- **Seeker Team:** 1 vehicle, 2+ players. Starts from the central point after the Hiders' 30-minute head start.
- For larger groups, add additional Hider or Seeker vehicles with their own Range Card budgets.

### 3. Distribute Cards
Each team draws a starting hand:
- **Hider Team:** 4 Evasion Cards, Range Cards totaling ~400 miles
- **Seeker Team:** 6 Location Cards, Range Cards totaling ~400 miles

Each team also receives County-Crossing Cards equal to the number of county boundaries in the play area (if using the Crossing Card optional rule).

### 4. Confirm Communications
- All players must be reachable by phone.
- Agree on a check-in interval (recommended: every 30 minutes, Seekers announce their current county).
- Agree on a safe word to pause the game for emergencies.

## Gameplay

### Game Flow
The game runs in **real time** — there are no formal turns. Players drive, plan, and communicate continuously. Cards can be played at any time, subject to the rules below.

### Hider's Goal
Stay hidden within the play area for the full game duration. Hiders choose their hiding spot during the 30-minute head start and may relocate at any time, but relocating costs Range Cards.

### Seeker's Goal
Physically locate the Hider's vehicle and make visual confirmation before time expires.

---

## Card Rules

### Location Cards (Seeker Team)
Seekers play Location Cards to ask the Hider team a **yes/no geographic question**. The Hider team must answer truthfully. Seekers may play at most **one Location Card per 10 minutes**.

**Examples of valid questions:**
- "Are you currently in [named county]?"
- "Are you north of [named highway / river / road]?"
- "Are you within a 15-minute drive of [named town]?"
- "Are you within 10 miles of [named landmark]?"
- "Are you in a town with a population below 500?"

Questions must be answerable with a clear yes or no based on the current map. Questions about the Hiders' future intentions ("Are you about to move?") are not valid.

### Evasion Cards (Hider Team)
Hiders play Evasion Cards to protect their position. Effects include:

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Silence** | Hiders do not have to answer the next Location Card played against them. |
| **Misdirect** | Hiders may give a false answer to the next Location Card (single use per game). |
| **Ghost Mode** | Seekers may not play Location Cards for 20 minutes. |
| **County Lock** | Seekers may not cross into a new county for 20 minutes. |
| **Road Block** | Seekers may not use a named road for 20 minutes. |
| **Smallest Town Shield** | While Hiders are in the smallest incorporated municipality of the play area, Seekers must spend an extra Range Card before playing a Location Card. |
| **Extra Time** | Add 20 minutes to the game clock (Hider benefit). |
| **Range Drain** | Seekers lose a Quarter Range immediately. |

### Range Cards (Both Teams)
Range Cards track each team's driving budget. See [Range Budgeting](./budgeting.md) for full details.

- Each team starts with Range Cards totaling ~400 miles.
- Deduct estimated mileage before each driving leg.
- A team that exhausts their Range Cards must draw a **Breakdown Card** (sit out 10 minutes, receive a Quarter Range) or forfeit the current leg.

### County-Crossing Cards (Optional Rule)
Each county boundary in the play area has one Crossing Card. A team must spend their Crossing Card for that boundary to legally cross it. Running out of Crossing Cards locks a team in their current county.

---

## Winning

- **Seekers win** if they physically locate and visually confirm the Hider's vehicle before time expires.
- **Hiders win** if they remain undetected until the game clock runs out.
- Either team wins instantly if the opposing team exhausts their Range Cards and cannot continue play.

### Tiebreaker
If game time expires and Seekers never made a confirmed sighting, Hiders win. If the game is called early (weather, emergency), the team with more Range Cards remaining wins.

## Optional Rules

### Multi-Seeker Teams
Add a second Seeker vehicle. Each vehicle has its own Range Card budget. Seekers may coordinate freely and share information from Location Cards.

### Time Penalties
If Seekers play a Location Card and the Hiders successfully invoke **Silence** or **Ghost Mode**, Seekers lose 10 minutes of game time.

### Night Games
Night games are possible but require all vehicles to stay on paved roads and check in every 15 minutes instead of 30. Flashlights and extra safety supplies are mandatory.

---

## Safety

- **Never** trespass on private property.
- Follow all traffic laws — the game is not worth a speeding ticket or accident.
- Carry a first aid kit, spare tire, and water.
- Share your live location with a non-playing emergency contact.
- Establish a safety check-in — if a team doesn't respond within 15 minutes of a scheduled check-in, pause the game and call them.
