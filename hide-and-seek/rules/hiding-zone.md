# Hiding Zone

> Part of the [Rural Hide and Seek Ruleset](./README.md)

Once the hider has reached their chosen hiding zone, the rules in [`transportation.md`](./transportation.md) no longer apply — hider movement and behavior inside the zone is governed by what's in this file.

---

## Use the official rulebook unchanged

The rural variant adapts personal vehicles to behave like trains and buses (see [`transportation.md`](./transportation.md) and [`../reference/transit-friction.md`](../reference/transit-friction.md)). Once the hider is inside their hiding zone, none of that adaptation is needed any more — there is no further travel-network friction to translate.

**For everything that happens at and inside the hiding zone, follow the official Hide + Seek rulebook exactly as written.** That includes:

- How the hiding zone is defined (anchor, radius, valid hiding spots within it).
- Hider movement inside the zone.
- The end-game trigger — when the seekers have officially entered the hiding zone, the hider may no longer move (must remain at a single publicly-accessible hiding spot).
- The "found" condition that resolves the round.
- Time-bonus scoring on resolution.

You'll need your physical copy of the rulebook for the canonical wording. If you don't have one, see the [Before you play](../../README.md#before-you-play) section of the top-level README for where to buy it; the relevant pages are summarized structurally (without quoting the text) in [`../reference/notes.md`](../reference/notes.md).

If a future rural-specific tweak to the hiding-zone rules turns out to be necessary, it will be added below this section. Until then, treat this file as a pointer back to the official rulebook.
