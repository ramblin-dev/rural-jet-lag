## Overview

Rural Jet Lag: Hide and Seek is a fan-made adaptation of Jet Lag: The Game's Hide and Seek format for rural environments. Players split into **Hiders** and **Seekers**, with the Hiders trying to stay hidden within a defined play area while Seekers use challenge cards to narrow down their location.

**Players:** 4–8 (2+ per team; minimum 2 teams)  
**Duration:** 3–8 hours  
**Play Area:** 25–100 mile radius from a central starting point  

---

## Setup

### 1. Define the Play Area
- Agree on a central starting point (a gas station or small town center works well).
- Define the outer boundary: county line(s), a circle of agreed radius, or named roads/highways.
- Mark prohibited zones (private property, etc.) on a shared map.
- Share the map with all players. Use Google My Maps, a printed county map, or a GPS app.

### 2. Assign Teams
- **Hider Team:** 1 vehicle, 2+ players. Starts 30 minutes before seekers.
- **Seeker Team:** 1 vehicle, 2+ players. Starts from the central point after a 30-minute head start.
- For larger groups, add additional hider or seeker teams (agreed rules for multi-team games apply).

### 3. Distribute Cards
Each team draws a starting hand:
- **Hider Team:** 4 Challenge Cards, 2 Gas Cards (totaling one full tank), 1 Powerup Card
- **Seeker Team:** 5 Challenge Cards, 2 Gas Cards (totaling one full tank), 1 Curse Card

Shuffle the remaining cards into a central deck (or use the provided card list in [`/assets/cards/`](../assets/cards/)).

### 4. Confirm Communications
- All players must be reachable by phone.
- Agree on a check-in interval (recommended: every 30 minutes, seekers announce current location).
- Agree on a safe word to pause the game for emergencies.

## Gameplay

### Turn Structure
The game runs in **real time** — there are no formal turns for movement. Players drive, plan, and communicate continuously. Cards are played at any time.

### Hider's Goal
Stay hidden within the play area for the full game duration (typically 4–6 hours).

### Seeker's Goal
Physically locate and visually confirm the Hider's vehicle before time expires.

### Playing Challenge Cards
Either team may play a Challenge Card at any time. The opposing team must complete the challenge to receive the stated reward (gas, information, extra time).

**Common Challenge Types:**
- **Navigation Challenge:** "Drive to the nearest grain elevator. Take a photo."
- **Trivia Challenge:** "Name the county seat of [adjacent county]."
- **Task Challenge:** "Visit the smallest incorporated town in the play area."
- **Social Challenge:** "Ask a local business owner for a recommendation. Show proof."

### Powerup Cards
Played by the Hider Team. Effects include:
- **Ghost Mode:** Seekers cannot ask about your location for 20 minutes.
- **Decoy:** Plant a fake location clue (tell seekers you are in a named town — they must drive there before getting another hint).
- **Fuel Drain:** Seekers lose a Quarter Tank.
- **Smallest Town:** Hiders gain the Smallest Town bonus (see Geography rules).

### Curse Cards
Played by the Seeker Team against the Hider Team. Effects include:
- **Detour Required:** Hiders must drive to a named landmark within 20 minutes.
- **Gravel Road Penalty:** Hiders must use a gravel road as their next movement leg.
- **County Line Pull:** Hiders must move to within 5 miles of a named county line within 30 minutes.
- **Gas Check:** Hiders must announce their current Gas Card balance.

---

## Winning

- **Seekers win** if they physically locate and visually confirm the Hider's vehicle before time expires.
- **Hiders win** if they remain undetected until the game clock runs out.
- Either team wins instantly if the opposing team runs out of Gas Cards and cannot continue.

### Tiebreaker
If game time expires and seekers never confirmed a sighting, hiders win. If the game is called early due to weather or emergency, the team with more Gas Cards remaining wins.

## Optional Rules

### Multi-Seeker Teams
Add a second Seeker vehicle. Each vehicle has its own Gas Card budget. Seekers may coordinate freely.

### Time Penalties
If a Seeker team plays a Curse Card and the Hider team completes the required task within the time limit, Seekers lose 15 minutes of game time (the clock is paused in hiders' favor).

### County-Crossing Cards
Replace the general "crossing is allowed with a card" rule with specific **County-Crossing Cards** — one per county boundary in the play area. Running out of County-Crossing Cards forces a team to stay in their current county.

### Night Games
Night games are possible but require all vehicles to stay on paved roads and check in every 15 minutes instead of 30. Flashlights and extra safety supplies are mandatory.

---

## Safety

- **Never** trespass on private property.
- Follow all traffic laws — the game is not worth a speeding ticket or accident.
- Carry a first aid kit, spare tire, and water.
- Share your live location with a non-playing emergency contact.
- Establish a safety check-in — if a team doesn't respond within 15 minutes of a check-in, pause the game and call them.
